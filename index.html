Privacy Policy for RealScan — AI Content Detector Last updated: March 20, 2026 Overview RealScan ("the Extension") is a Chrome browser extension that detects whether content is AI-generated. This privacy policy explains what data the Extension accesses, how it is used, and what is stored. Data We Do Not Collect RealScan does not collect, transmit, store, or share any personal information. We do not operate any servers or databases. We have no ability to identify who you are or track your browsing activity. Data Processed Locally The following data is stored exclusively on your device using Chrome's local storage (chrome.storage.local) and never leaves your device except as described below: Your Google Gemini API key, saved only so you don't have to re-enter it Your preferred scan mode (Text, Image, or Auto) Data Sent to Third Parties When you initiate a scan, the content you select (text or image) is sent directly from your browser to the Google Gemini API (generativelanguage.googleapis.com) for AI detection analysis. This transmission is governed by Google's Privacy Policy. RealScan does not intercept, log, or store this data at any point. No other third parties receive any data. Permissions Justification activeTab — to access the page you are currently viewing when you activate a scan scripting — to inject the selection overlay onto the active page storage — to save your API key and preferences locally on your device Host permission (generativelanguage.googleapis.com) — to send selected content to the Gemini API for analysis Host permission () — to allow the scan overlay to work on any webpage you choose to analyze Children's Privacy RealScan is not directed at children under the age of 13 and does not knowingly collect any information from children.
